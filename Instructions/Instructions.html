The Experiment

Stage 1: Project selection
You will be provided with an initial endowment and presented with two alternative projects. You will be asked to choose the project you would like to invest in. At this stage you are basically making a random choice, as all the relevant information regarding costs and potential revenues will become available to you at the following stage.

Stage 2: Project continuation
At this stage, you will be informed about the investment cost you have incurred (initial investment cost) and the revenue gained as a result of your initial investment choice (intermediate revenue). Moreover, you will be informed about the probability of success of both your chosen and perhaps your unchosen project option. You will be asked to decide whether you want to finalize your chosen project by making an additional investment or to abandon it and keep your endowment as it has been shaped up to that point (current balance).

Project details

Your initial endowment is $10.
The intial investment cost can be either high or low and may exceed your initial endowment. However, the intermediate revenue will guarantee that under no circumstances will you end up with a negative balance.

The probability of project success can be either 25% or 75%.
Both values for the initial investment cost and the probability of success are equally likely to occur.

Your payoff

If you choose to abandon the project:

Expected payoff = Initial endowment - Initial investment cost + Intermediate revenue


If you choose to carry on with the project:

Expected payoff = Initial endowment - Initial investment cost + Intermediate revenue - Additional investment cost + Probability of project success * Additional revenue


You will be informed about the outcome of your investment right after making your choice at Stage 2. After the required sample is gathered, 5 participants will be payed their payoff in addition to their participation fee.

Chances are high, so make sure your decision represents your true preferences!

Press "Next" to start the experiment!
